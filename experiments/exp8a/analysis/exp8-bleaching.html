Intensify of degree adverbs: Experiment 8

erin

I ran 20 participants on an experiment where I showed them comics where either the word truly or the word very was used 11 times. The target question was:

I also asked how many times participants thought the speaker would use truly and very.

It seems like the stories I gave did not push around peoples expectations about how many times Jim would use the words very and truly, although it seems to be moving in the right direction.

Ill try more repetition next time.

As a result of the frequency estimates not changing much, we cant expect the prices to change very much either. And indeed they dont.

There also doesnt seem to be any correlation between peoples frequency estimates and their price estimates. To the extent that there is any relationship at all, it appears to be kind of positive.
